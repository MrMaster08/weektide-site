Questions

The honest answers.

Is Tempo really free?
Yes — the free app is the whole core, not a demo. Unlimited tasks, lists and tags, the natural-language quick add, Apple Calendar on the same grid, habits with streaks and heatmaps, the entire Grove, widgets and the menu-bar quick add. Nothing in Free expires.
What does Plus add?
The connected layer: cloud sync across your devices, sharing with the person you plan with, Google Calendar and work-calendar integrations, task templates and saved filters, and premium Grove scenes. US$1.99 a month, after a 30-day free trial of everything.
Why the extra step on first launch?
Tempo is a direct download rather than an App Store build, so macOS asks you to confirm the first launch. Open Tempo once (macOS shows a notice), then go to System Settings → Privacy & Security, scroll down and click Open Anyway, and confirm. You do this exactly once — every launch after that is normal.
When is the iPhone app coming?
It’s built — tasks, calendar, habits and the Grove all run on iPhone today — and it arrives with the App Store release. With sync turned on, your Mac and iPhone stay in step from day one.
Where does my data live?
On your device. Tempo works completely offline, and nothing leaves your Mac unless you turn on sync or sharing — both optional, both under your control. The full detail is in the privacy policy.
