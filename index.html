Questions

The honest answers.

Is Weektide really free?
Yes. The free app is the whole core. You get unlimited tasks, lists and tags, the natural-language quick add, Apple Calendar on the same grid, habits with streaks and heatmaps, the entire Grove, widgets and the menu-bar quick add. Nothing in Free expires.
What does Plus add?
Plus adds cloud sync across your devices, sharing with the person you plan with, “Show as Busy” for tasks you keep private, task templates and saved filters, and premium Grove scenes. US$1.99 a month, after a 30-day free trial of everything.
Why the extra step on first launch?
Weektide is a direct download rather than an App Store build, so macOS asks you to confirm the first launch. Open Weektide once (macOS shows a notice), then go to System Settings → Privacy & Security, scroll down and click Open Anyway, and confirm. You do this exactly once. Every launch after that is normal.
When is the iPhone app coming?
It’s built: tasks, calendar, habits and the Grove all run on iPhone today, and it arrives with the App Store release. With sync turned on, your Mac and iPhone stay in step from day one.
Where is Weektide heading?
Toward a planner that understands hard weeks. It will pull commitments out of rosters and emails as proposals you approve, plan around real energy rather than empty calendar space, and recover the day calmly when it collapses. Nothing is marketed before it ships: the Building next page tracks what’s shipped, what’s in progress and what’s planned, and What is a shared life planner? explains the idea Weektide is built around.
Where does my data live?
On your device. Weektide works completely offline, and nothing leaves your Mac unless you turn on sync or sharing. Both are optional and under your control. The full detail is in the privacy policy.
