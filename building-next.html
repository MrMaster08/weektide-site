The road ahead

Building next.

Weektide is built by one person, in public, around one rule: nothing is marketed before it ships. This page is the honest ledger of what's in the app today, what's being built now, and what's planned. No dates, because dates from a one-person team are fiction. Just the order things ship in.

Shipped Building Planned

The foundation

Shipped

Everything on the homepage is in the app today. It's the part you can count on.

  • One grid for the whole week: tasks, events, habits and focus time together, with durations that block real time.
  • A calendar built for changing weeks: a full year down to a single day, fast enough that re-entering a new fortnight isn't a slog.
  • Sharing by invitation: connect with whoever you plan with, share exactly the lists you choose, hand tasks over, and follow what changed.
  • Offline first: everything works with no connection and syncs when you're back online.
  • The Grove: focus sessions that grow a forest, kept in your personal space. Shared spaces never have leaderboards.

Lists that reach the calendar

Shipped

A shared list is only half the job. The other half is time. Here's what connects them:

  • Give any list item a date and it lands on the calendar in its list's colour, on both calendars if the list is shared.
  • Repeating list items: milk comes back to the grocery list next week on its own.
  • Whose turn it is, visible: when someone takes a task, the calendar shows who has it, so bin night has a name on it.

Commitment capture

Shipped · v0

Weektide reads pasted text and turns the commitments it finds into proposals you approve. It never changes your plan on its own. Paste an email, an announcement, or a roster into “Add from Text” and Weektide lists the dated commitments it finds, on your device, for you to approve one by one. Photos and PDFs come next. The person who notices everything shouldn't also have to be the one who types it all in.

Planning against real capacity

Building

A two-hour gap after a long shift is not two hours of energy. The first slice shipped: every calendar day now shows how much it really holds, and moving something in the re-plan shows what the target day already carries. Next comes the harder part: commutes, recovery, and the gap between time you have and time you can use.

Recovery, when the day collapses

Shipped

Every planner is designed for the day that goes well. Instead of a wall of overdue red, Weektide asks a calm question: "Today didn't go to plan?" Then it gives you one quiet review of everything still hanging over today, where each thing can move (with the target day's real load beside it), shrink, be handed to the person you plan with, or be dropped. A bad day is something to recover from.

Sharing that goes further

Shipped · growing

Two rungs landed together. The privacy gradient: mark a private task “Show as Busy” and whoever you share with sees that the time is taken, without seeing what it is. And more than one person: connect with each of the people you plan with — family, a housemate, anyone. Each connection is its own invitation, and everyone sees only what you mark shared. (Everyone needs the current version.) Next: per-person sharing choices, so different people can see different lists.

Calendars beyond Apple

Planned

Weektide reads Apple Calendar today, and for one calendar life that's enough. But plenty of weeks are split across two: a personal calendar and the one your work or uni lives in. Bringing Google and work calendars onto the same grid is on the list, and it arrives the way everything here does — when the sync is solid, not when the bullet point would look good on a pricing page.

Rules the roadmap follows

Features change; these don't.

  • Nothing changes your plan silently. Anything Weektide suggests, whether a captured commitment or a re-plan, is a proposal you approve.
  • Sharing is an invitation you accept. It's never an account you're conscripted into. Both sides say yes, and either side can leave.
  • A bad day is something to recover from. No guilt-red backlogs. Recovery is built into the app.
  • Gamification stays in your personal Grove. No points, streaks or scoreboards in shared spaces. Sharing is for working together.
  • What you pay for today never gets taken away. Early supporters keep their price for life, and your data exports free, always.
